Figure skating has always been an integral part of my life. My parents both coach skating full time and my sister is also a skater. Most people understand that it takes years of training and dedication to become a competitive skater, but what’s less obvious is the family investment, community engagement, and physical sacrifice involved. It takes an extraordinary number of hours to perfect jumping, spinning, and other advanced elements which popular culture and Olympic media often make seem more commonplace than they actually are. No amount of money or elite coaching can buy a jump–you have to work for it. For most of my life I have had ice time before and after school totaling about 5 hours per day. I feel honored to have begun coaching and teaching alongside my parents, adding to my dedication and passion for the sport. Figure skating has been the structure that taught me discipline and community. Skating has taught me self-pride, presentation, humility and etiquette. It has also given me mental toughness and an ability to literally brush myself off and keep going.
